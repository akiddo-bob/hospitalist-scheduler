Block 3 Scheduling Rules

Reference guide for the scheduling engine constraints and logic.

Site Assignment

1.Providers are only assigned to sites where they have a non-zero allocation percentage.
2.Smaller sites are filled first (to capacity), then Cooper absorbs the remainder.
3.Cooper's full demand (26 weekday / 19 weekend) is the target. Shortfalls are expected — moonlighters fill the gap.
4.Providers stay at one site for a complete week+weekend stretch when possible.

Scheduling Constraints

1.No more than 2 consecutive active weeks (hard cap). A 3rd consecutive week would create a 19+ day stretch.
2.12-day stretches (2 consecutive weeks) are penalized but allowed when obligations require it.
3.Weekend assignments prefer pairing with the same site as the weekday assignment for the same week number.
4.Standalone weekends in back-to-back weeks are discouraged.

Availability & Time Off

1.All provider schedule requests (time off) are honored — never overridden.
2.If honoring requests means a provider cannot be fully scheduled, this is documented with the reason.
3.Providers are never assigned to weeks/weekends where they are marked unavailable.

Capacity & Obligations

1.Each provider owes a specific number of weeks and weekends (from their contract, minus prior blocks).
2.Remaining obligations are rounded UP to the next whole number (e.g., 14.6 weeks = 15 weeks owed).
3.Night-shift-only providers are excluded. Split providers (nights + days) are scheduled for their day obligations.

Distribution & Fairness

1.Assignments are distributed evenly across the full block — no front-loading.
2.Week processing order is randomized to prevent early-block bias.
3.Ideal spacing is calculated per provider to evenly spread their assignments.
4.Rebalancing passes attempt to fill contractual gaps after the initial assignment.
5.Gap-leveling smooths out per-site fill counts across weeks (move from surplus to shortfall weeks).
6.Cross-site gap fill places remaining unassigned providers into the neediest site+period combinations.
